Goals and Objectives

Short-Term Goals

  1. Complete my Bachelor’s degree in Computer Information Systems with a concentration in Networking and Cybersecurity at Northwestern State University.
  2. Earn at least one CompTIA certification (Security+ or Network+) within the next 36 months.
  3. Apply for at least one internal position or advancement opportunity at Cleco that better utilizes both my E&I experience and new CIS education.
  4. Maintain a GPA of 3.0 or higher while balancing full-time work and family responsibilities.
  5. Update and strengthen my LinkedIn profile to better reflect my combined industrial and cybersecurity education.

Long-Term Goals

  1. Transition into a role focused on Operational Technology (OT) security, SCADA systems, or industrial control system reliability.
  2. Advance into a supervisory or management position such as E&I Maintenance Supervisor or SCADA Operations Lead.
  3. Earn additional professional certifications in cybersecurity and industrial control systems (such as CISSP, CCNA, or IEC 62443-related credentials).
  4. Build a professional network that includes both industrial control system professionals and cybersecurity specialists.
  5. Become recognized within my organization as someone who can effectively communicate between operations/maintenance teams and IT/cybersecurity teams.

Reflection

Returning to college after such a long time away has been a big adjustment, but I’ve had so much support along the way. The CIS program is a great fit for my career goals. The NSU faculty and staff have been very helpful and responsive every time I have reached out for support. My employer, Cleco, has also played a big role by introducing me to the CompeteLA program and reimbursing most of my tuition after I pass each class. Their reimbursement incentive has removed a lot of financial pressure and stress throughout this process. My U.S. Navy experience helped prepare me for these challenges because it taught me discipline and how to learn new technical skills under pressure. Between NSU, Cleco’s support, and the structure I gained from my military service, I feel like I have a solid foundation to finish my degree, earn some certifications, and move into a role that better uses both my industrial experience and my networking and cybersecurity knowledge.
