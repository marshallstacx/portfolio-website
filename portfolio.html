University of the People

University of the People is a tuition-free, accredited online university designed to make higher education accessible to people who might not otherwise be able to attend college. After being out of school for more than ten years, I enrolled at UoPeople to ease back into academics. The courses were challenging and required strong time management and dedication. Through my classes there, I significantly improved my academic writing skills, learned how to properly format reference lists in APA style, and gained confidence in incorporating citations into my work. This experience gave me a strong foundation and made my transition to Northwestern State University much smoother.

Northwestern State University

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Information Systems with a concentration in Networking and Cybersecurity at Northwestern State University. The online program has been a great fit for my schedule as a full-time working parent. The faculty and staff have been extremely supportive and responsive, which has helped build my confidence as a returning adult student. I have already gained valuable knowledge in web development, networking concepts, and cybersecurity fundamentals. Being part of NSU has strengthened my commitment to completing my degree and advancing my career.

Cleco and CompeteLA

My employer, Cleco, introduced me to the CompeteLA program, a Louisiana initiative that helps adult learners return to college and finish their degrees. CompeteLA provided guidance through the entire process of selecting a school, applying, and understanding financial aid options. The program also offers reduced tuition rates at participating state colleges. Cleco further supports my education by reimbursing 85% of my tuition cost after I successfully complete each course. This partnership has removed many financial and logistical barriers and has made it possible for me to pursue my degree while continuing to work full time and raise my family.

Cyber Demons – NSU Computer Information Systems Club

Cyber Demons is the CIS club at Northwestern State University. The club focuses on networking, cybersecurity, and programming through meetings, demonstrations, and hands-on activities. I have participated in several club events where we discuss current topics in the field, work on projects, and even play cybersecurity-related games. These meetings have given me the opportunity to network with other CIS students, alumni, and faculty members. Being involved in Cyber Demons has helped me feel more connected to the university community and has expanded my understanding of real-world applications in cybersecurity and information systems.

U.S. Navy – Sonar Technician

I served as a Sonar Technician in the U.S. Navy from 2002 to 2008, including five years aboard the USS Nicholas FFG-47. During my service I performed routine and corrective maintenance on complex electronic, acoustic, and weapons systems, supervising teams of up to 15 personnel, and completed multiple extended deployments. My time in the Navy played a major role in shaping who I am today. It gave me strong technical troubleshooting skills, taught me how to work under pressure, and helped me develop leadership and discipline. These experiences laid the foundation for my career in electrical, electronics, and instrumentation work and continue to support me as I transition into roles involving operational technology and cybersecurity.
